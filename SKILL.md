# Workplace Resilience: Tactical Skill

> **Ground rule**: Every hostile move leaves a trace. Your job is to make that trace work for you.

---

## Part 1 — Recognize the Attack

### Cold Violence (Silent Exclusion)

Deliberate, deniable, and designed to make you feel invisible without giving you anything concrete to report.

**Signs in chat**
- Your messages get zero replies in channels where others receive instant responses
- You're not @mentioned when the whole team is, then @mentioned when blame needs assigning
- Decisions are announced in channels or group chats you weren't in
- Key information is shared in a side-thread or private group that excludes only you
- Your ideas go unacknowledged in a thread; someone else restates the same idea minutes later and gets praised

**Signs in person / meetings**
- Invited to a meeting but talked over, interrupted, or never given the floor
- Action items distributed to everyone except you — you're not assigned work, not trusted, and then blamed for not contributing
- Your questions are answered with monosyllables while others get engaged responses

### Digital Platform Attack Patterns

#### Slack

| Attack | What You See | What's Really Happening |
|--------|-------------|------------------------|
| **Channel Ghosting** | Your message sits unanswered; identical messages from others get replies within minutes | Coordinated group ignore — often tacitly led by one senior person |
| **Selective @mention** | `@channel` for good news; your name specifically tagged for blame or overload | You're "in" for liability, "out" for credit |
| **Private Channel Exile** | Decisions reference conversations you weren't part of | Power hoarding through information asymmetry |
| **Thread Burial** | You post something important; someone immediately starts a new top-level message | Your visibility deliberately suppressed |
| **DM Trap** | Pulled into a private DM for something that should be in a project channel | Removing witnesses before delivering bad news or making unfair demands |
| **Read-and-Ignore** | Green active dot; "Seen" on your DM; silence for hours | Power assertion — you don't deserve a response |
| **Reaction Bombing** | Your proposal gets a string of 🤔😬 from a clique | Group signaling without verbal accountability — forces self-censorship |
| **Status Shield** | Person sets Do Not Disturb exactly when you message them, responds to others publicly | Targeted unavailability |

#### Feishu / Lark

| Attack | What You See | What's Really Happening |
|--------|-------------|------------------------|
| **Group Chat Exile** | Project chats exist without you; you learn about them from meeting notes | Deliberate information gatekeeping |
| **Approval Weaponization** | Your requests sit pending for days; peers' requests process same-day | Control via bureaucratic delay |
| **Permission Lock** | Shared docs are view-only for you, editable for everyone else | Excluding you from collaborative decisions |
| **OKR Ambush** | Negative comments added to your review close to the submission deadline | Timed reputational attack when you can't effectively respond |
| **Meeting Link Omission** | Calendar invite exists; link only shared in a group you're not in | Plausible-deniability exclusion |
| **Read Receipt Gaslighting** | Platform shows they read your message; they later claim "I never saw it" | Exploiting platform ambiguity to deny responsibility |
| **Credit Erasure** | Team wins posted in Moments/Circle; your contribution not mentioned | Soft deletion from the win narrative |

#### Microsoft Teams

| Attack | What You See | What's Really Happening |
|--------|-------------|------------------------|
| **Silent Channel Removal** | You can no longer access a channel you were in | Exclusion via admin action with no explanation |
| **Read-Denial** | Message clearly delivered and read; person claims ignorance | Using interface ambiguity as cover |
| **Meeting Shadow** | You're on the invite but a parallel chat thread runs without you during the call | Two-tier participation |
| **Decision Archaeology** | Major decisions cited that happened in chats you weren't in | Retroactive justification using inaccessible records |

### Manipulation Tactics (PUA-Style)

These are psychological moves, not just communication failures. Recognizing them breaks their power.

**Love Bombing → Devaluation**
Excessive early praise, "you're the only one I trust," special treatment — then sudden withdrawal, criticism, and cold shoulder. The cycle repeats to keep you seeking approval.
- *Tell*: the praise feels disproportionate; the withdrawal is sudden and unexplained; you find yourself working harder to get the "good version" back

**Gaslighting**
"That never happened." "You're too sensitive." "You misunderstood me completely." Designed to make you distrust your own memory and perception.
- *Tell*: you leave conversations confused about what was actually said; you start apologizing for things you didn't do

**Triangulation**
"[Other person] handles this so much better." Creating competition and comparison to destabilize your confidence and turn colleagues against each other.
- *Tell*: you're being compared to a specific person repeatedly; that person is positioned as the benchmark you fail to meet

**Intermittent Reinforcement**
Unpredictable kindness and cruelty. Sometimes warm, sometimes brutal — no consistent trigger. Creates anxiety and dependency.
- *Tell*: you're walking on eggshells; you feel relief when they're nice and dread when they're not; you can't predict interactions

**Isolation**
"I don't think [Colleague] really understands you." Subtly discouraging your other relationships to increase dependence on the bully.
- *Tell*: you find yourself confiding less in colleagues; your external relationships have drifted; one person has become your main source of validation

## Part 2 — The Counterpunch Playbook

Ten deployable moves. Each is professional, evidence-creating, and designed to shift power back to you.

---

### Move 1 — The Paper Trail Maneuver
**Trigger**: A verbal or DM agreement you expect to be denied later.

Within 30 minutes of the conversation, post publicly (channel or email):

> *"Quick recap of what [Name] and I just discussed: [Decision A was made / Action B is on [Name] / Action C is on me by [date]]. Flag me if I've captured anything incorrectly."*

**Why it works**: They must explicitly contradict a public, timestamped record to dispute it. Most won't. If they do, their contradiction is also documented.

---

### Move 2 — The Factual Mirror
**Trigger**: A hostile, dismissive, or false statement made about you in chat or in a meeting.

> *"I want to make sure I understand — you're saying [restate their exact words]. Is that accurate?"*

**In chat**: Quote their message using the platform's reply function, then ask the same question.

**Why it works**: Forces commitment. They affirm it (now documented), soften it (attack reduced), or deny it (inconsistency revealed). You said nothing aggressive — only asked for clarity.

---

### Move 3 — The Precision Timestamp
**Trigger**: Gaslighting — *"That never happened" / "I never said that" / "You misunderstood."*

> *"I have the message from [date] at [time] in #[channel]. The exact text was: '[quote]'. Happy to share the permalink/screenshot."*

**Critical rule**: Never argue memory. Go to receipts in the same breath. Calm tone. Zero apology.

**If they persist**:
> *"I understand we may recall it differently. I'd suggest we review the record together with [Manager/HR] to get aligned."*

---

### Move 4 — The Channel Redirect
**Trigger**: Pulled into a DM for something that should have witnesses.

**In the DM**:
> *"This is an important point — let me loop in [Manager/Colleague] so we're all on the same page."*

Add them to the thread. Or, if you'd rather not escalate yet, post a public summary after:

> *"Following up on a conversation [Name] and I had — we discussed [X] and agreed [Y]. Sharing in #[channel] so the team has visibility."*

**Why it works**: You're being transparent and collaborative. They lose the no-witness advantage. Any further hostile moves are now made in public.

---

### Move 5 — The Redirect to Process
**Trigger**: Pressured to act improperly, skip steps, or comply with a verbal instruction that leaves no trail.

> *"Happy to move on this quickly. To make sure we're covered, can you put the request through [official channel / approval workflow / ticket system]? I'll action it immediately once it's there."*

**Why it works**: You're not refusing. You're protecting both parties — a framing they can't easily object to. If they refuse to document it, that refusal is the evidence.

---

### Move 6 — The Strategic Silence Break
**Trigger**: Systematically ignored in a channel across multiple messages.

Stop posting to the void. Send one targeted direct message:

> *"Hey [Name], a few messages of mine in #[channel] seem to have gotten lost. I don't want to be a blocker — can you let me know [specific question]?"*

**Escalation rule**: If this message is also ignored within 24 hours, forward the full thread to your manager:
> *"I've tried [X] times to get clarity on [issue] in #[channel]. Can you help unblock me?"*

---

### Move 7 — The CC Cascade
**Trigger**: Information is withheld from you and shared with peers; you need to surface the gap without a direct confrontation.

When you eventually receive any update, reply and add your manager:

> *"Thanks for the update. I've added [Manager] to this thread for visibility so we're all aligned. Happy to join the relevant group chat going forward as well."*

**Why it works**: Reasonable. Collaborative. Makes future omissions look deliberate rather than accidental. Sets up a pattern of visibility.

---

### Move 8 — The Boundary with Teeth
**Trigger**: Consistent disrespect, sarcasm, or dismissiveness in chat or meetings.

In writing, after the incident — referencing it specifically:

> *"I want to flag that the comment '[exact quote]' in #[channel / meeting name] on [date] felt disrespectful. I'd like our communication to stay professional. If there's a work concern, I'm happy to address it directly."*

**Rules**:
- Quote exactly — no paraphrase
- Name the platform, channel, and date
- No emotion words ("I felt hurt") — describe behavior only
- End with an open door (shows you're reasonable; they look unreasonable if they escalate)

---

### Move 9 — The Volunteer Witness
**Trigger**: A 1:1 meeting is coming that you expect to be disputed or used against you.

Before the meeting, in writing:

> *"Looking forward to our discussion at [time]. Would it be helpful to have [HR / trusted colleague] join? I find it useful to have a third person for alignment on action items."*

**Outcomes**:
- They agree → you have a witness
- They decline → that refusal is worth noting in your log
- Either way, send a detailed summary email within 30 minutes of the meeting

---

### Move 10 — The Defuse and Document
**Trigger**: An outburst, personal attack, or inappropriate comment in a group setting.

**In the moment** — calm, short, then stop:
> *"I'd like to continue this when we've both had a chance to step back. I'll follow up in writing."*

Do not engage further. Leave if possible.

**Immediately after**, in writing:
> *"Following up on our exchange in [meeting/channel] at [time]: [factual description of what was said, quoted where possible]. I'd like to address [specific issue] professionally. Can we schedule time with [Manager/HR]?"*

**Why it works**: You de-escalated publicly (looks good for you). You documented factually (protects you). You moved it to a formal track (serious consequences for them if repeated).

## Part 3 — Evidence System

### What to Capture

```
Date/Time:        [exact platform timestamp]
Platform:         [Slack / Feishu / Teams / email / in-person]
Who:              [full names and roles]
What happened:    [exact quotes — not paraphrases, not summaries]
Witnesses:        [names, or "none"]
My response:      [what you said or did]
Evidence saved:   [screenshot filename / permalink / email forward ref]
Work impact:      [how it affected deliverables, deadlines, or your role]
```

### Platform Evidence Methods

| Platform | Best Evidence Method |
|----------|---------------------|
| Slack | Screenshot + "Copy link to message" permalink; export DM history via Settings |
| Feishu | Screenshot group membership panel, permission pages, approval history, OKR threads |
| Teams | Screenshot channel membership + chat; use "Share to Outlook" to cross-post to email |
| Email | Forward to personal email immediately; do not rely on company archive access |
| In-person | Written memo to self within 1 hour; send follow-up email to attendees as "meeting recap" |

### Storage Rules
- Store **outside company systems** — personal cloud storage or personal email
- Keep **chronological order** — date-named folders or a running log file
- Use **factual language only** — no emotional descriptions; write what happened, not how it felt
- **Back up weekly**

## Part 4 — Escalation Path

### When to Escalate
- Behavior continues after direct counterpunch
- Multiple people are being affected
- Your performance reviews or compensation are being impacted
- Physical or mental health is affected
- Retaliation occurs after you raise concerns

### Escalation Sequence

**Level 1 — Direct (with documentation)**
Address the person directly. Use Move 8 (Boundary with Teeth) in writing. Document their response.

**Level 2 — Manager**
> *"I want to share a pattern I've been experiencing and get your guidance on how to handle it."*
Bring your incident log. Present facts chronologically. Request specific action. Follow up in writing.

**Level 3 — HR Formal Complaint**
File with your full documentation. Request a written investigation timeline. Ask explicitly about anti-retaliation protections. Keep copies of everything HR sends you.

**Level 4 — Senior Leadership**
If HR is unresponsive or complicit. Contact skip-level manager or executive sponsor with a concise, evidence-based summary.

**Level 5 — External**
- Labor board complaint
- Legal consultation
- EEOC filing (discrimination-based cases)
- Union representative (if applicable)

### Escalation Email Template

```
Subject: Formal Complaint — Workplace Conduct, [Your Name]

Dear [Name],

I am writing to formally report a pattern of behavior by [Name/Role]
that has created a hostile work environment and impacted my ability
to perform my role.

Summary:
[2–3 sentences: what has been happening, since when]

Documentation:
I have attached an incident log covering [date range], including
[screenshots / chat exports / email threads / witness names].

Impact:
[Specific effects: missed information, exclusion from decisions,
performance review discrepancy, health impact]

Previous Attempts to Resolve:
[Outline direct conversation attempts, dates, outcomes]

Requested Action:
[Specific: investigation, mediation, channel access restored,
transfer, formal warning to the other party]

I am available to discuss at your earliest convenience.

[Your Name]
[Date]
```
